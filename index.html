Design is Medicine

Life first. Health a distant second.
by Juhan Sonin, juhan@goinvo.com

Download Presentation

It's time to design products to capture life and physiologic signs invisibly… usually through non-invasive sensors that don't require a single drop of blood, but just whiffs and sniffs. And when it is visible, it must be designed to feel wonderful.

All the data is collected… automagically. It makes us think more about Life and less about "health" and "security." Which then in turn allows us to focus all of our energy on our dreams (versus fat fingering daily food diaries).

The data enables us to live aware of our health. As soon as our life information becomes quantified, not in having to "physically-type-in-my-daily-activities-and-look-things-up", but in a "here's-my-daily-report-and-your recent-eating-habits-have-taken-2-days-off-your-life-expectancy", that's when the world changes. But not before.

About Juhan Sonin

Juhan Sonin is the Creative Director of Involution Studios and has produced work recognized by the New York Times, Newsweek, BBC International, Billboard Magazine, and National Public Radio (NPR). He has spent time at Apple, the National Center for Supercomputing Applications (NCSA), and MITRE. Juhan lectures on design and engineering at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT).
